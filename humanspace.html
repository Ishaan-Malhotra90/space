Humans & Space

Human exploration in space embodies our relentless quest for knowledge and our unyielding spirit of adventure. From the historic moon landings to ongoing missions to Mars and beyond, we continuously push the boundaries of our understanding. Collaborative efforts and private initiatives drive us towards establishing sustainable settlements on celestial bodies, while unlocking scientific breakthroughs and inspiring future generations to explore the vast wonders of the cosmos.

List of Missions by National Aeronautics and Space Administration

  • 1961-1972
    Apollo Program
    Series of manned lunar missions, including the first manned Moon landing in 1969.
  • 1975
    Viking 1
    First successful mission to land and operate on Mars.
  • 1977
    Voyager 1
    Explored Jupiter, Saturn, and continued into interstellar space.
  • 1990
    Hubble Space Telescope
    Revolutionized astronomy with its stunning images and observations of distant galaxies and cosmic phenomena.
  • 1997
    Cassini-Huygens
    Orbited Saturn and sent a probe to its moon, Titan.
  • 1997
    Mars Pathfinder
    Landed a rover on Mars to gather data and study the planet's surface.
  • 1999
    Chandra X-ray Observatory
    Studied X-ray emissions from high-energy regions in the universe, such as black holes and supernova remnants.
  • 2003
    Mars Exploration Rovers (Spirit and Opportunity)
    Explored the Martian surface and provided valuable data on Mars' geology and climate.
  • 2003
    Mars Express
    Studied the Martian atmosphere, surface, and subsurface from orbit.
  • 2003
    Spitzer Space Telescope
    Observed the universe in infrared light, revealing new insights into star formation and exoplanets.
  • 2004
    Rosetta
    Orbited and landed a probe on a comet to study its composition and evolution.
  • 2006
    New Horizons
    First mission to explore Pluto and its moons, providing detailed images and data.
  • 2009
    Kepler Space Telescope
    Discovered thousands of exoplanets, revolutionizing our understanding of other solar systems.
  • 2011
    Curiosity Rover
    Investigated Mars' habitability and potential for supporting microbial life.
  • 2011
    Juno
    Orbiting Jupiter to study its atmosphere, magnetic field, and auroras.
  • 2018
    Insight
    Studying the interior of Mars to gain insights into the planet's formation and evolution.
  • 2018
    Parker Solar Probe
    Approaching the Sun to study its outer atmosphere and solar wind.
  • 2018
    Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS)
    Studying the interior of Mars to gain insights into the planet's formation and evolution.
  • 2021
    James Webb Space Telescope
    Searching for exoplanets using the transit method to detect periodic dips in starlight.
