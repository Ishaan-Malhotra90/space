International Space Station

The International Space Station (ISS) is a multinational research laboratory orbiting Earth, where astronauts conduct experiments and study the effects of space travel. It symbolizes international cooperation and paves the way for future missions to the Moon and Mars.

  • Construction - Collaborative project
  • Size - Approximately 109 meters
  • Length - 420,000 kilograms
  • Capacity - Up to 6 astronauts
  • Altitude - Approximately 400 kilometers
  • Orbit - Around the Earth

More about International Space Station:

1. The ISS is a collaborative project involving space agencies from 15 countries, including NASA (United States), Roscosmos (Russia), ESA (European Space Agency), JAXA (Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency), and CSA (Canadian Space Agency).

2. The ISS exemplifies international cooperation in space exploration. Different countries contribute modules, equipment, and resources to the station's operations, maintenance, and research activities.

3. : The ISS has been continuously inhabited since November 2000, and it has been visited by astronauts from various countries during its existence. It serves as a platform for testing technologies and systems that will be essential for future long-duration space missions, including those to the Moon and Mars.

4. The ISS is supported by regular resupply missions that deliver food, water, equipment, and experiments. Cargo spacecraft, such as SpaceX's Dragon and Northrop Grumman's Cygnus, are used for resupply missions.
